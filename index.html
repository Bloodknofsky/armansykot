Exploring the strange, beautiful logic of the quantum world — where entanglement and superposition aren't science fiction, they're the substrate.

I'm a Graduate Student Researcher in the Quantum Sensing and Imaging Lab (QuSI) at Korea University, pursuing an Integrated MS-PhD in Physics with a major in Quantum Information Physics. My current research focuses on quantum computation based on spin qubits.

Previously, I was a Research Assistant at the NSU Center of Quantum Computing (NCQC), where I worked on quantum cryptography and key distribution protocols.
